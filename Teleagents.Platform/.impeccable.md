## Design Context

### Users
Operations managers and contact center leads who review call performance and agent health during business hours, at a desk. They need to understand metrics quickly, spot anomalies, and make staffing or config decisions. They are not developers. Speed of comprehension is everything — they have limited time and high accountability.

### Brand Personality
Modern and confident. Enterprise-grade without being stuffy. Three words: **clear, assured, capable**. The interface should feel like it was built by people who know what they're doing — not flashy, not tentative. Stripe/Clerk lineage: polished spacing, strong typographic hierarchy, nothing wasted.

### Aesthetic Direction
Light mode primary. Clean, refined, data-forward. Reference: Stripe Dashboard, Clerk — precise spacing, confident neutrals, subtle borders, real hierarchy through weight and space rather than color. Anti-references: anything that looks like a template (icon grid cards, hero metric layouts with gradients, decorative sparklines). The palette is an olive-tinted OKLCH system — warm near-black, off-white, warm grays — lean into that warmth rather than fighting it with blue-gray.

### Design Principles
1. **Hierarchy over decoration** — communicate importance through size, weight, and space. Never add visual chrome to compensate for weak structure.
2. **Data at a glance** — the most important numbers should be readable without squinting. Managers open this page to know the answer in 5 seconds.
3. **Restrained confidence** — every element should feel intentional and earn its place. No placeholders, no lorem ipsum aesthetics.
4. **Consistent rhythm** — varied spacing that creates a beat: tight within groups, generous between sections.
5. **Trust through detail** — small things done right (optical alignment, consistent copy casing, proper focus states) signal that the product is reliable.
